## **“We took direct action to repair the damage we caused, speaking face-to-face or changing our behavior, except when doing so would cause further drama, stress, or hurt to others.”**

### **Amends Means Making Things Right**

In high school, words can feel cheap because people say "sorry" without changing their actions. Step Nine isn't just about apologizing; it's about **repairing**. If you let down a teammate, you don't just text them an excuse; you show up early to the next practice ready to work. True autonomy means your actions match your words.

### **The "No Drama" Rule**

The most critical part of this step is knowing when _not_ to push. If approaching someone face-to-face is going to spark a rumor mill, stir up old group-chat drama, or make the other person feel uncomfortable, you must choose a different path. Sometimes, the most mature way to make amends is **silent, permanent behavioral change**—showing respect by giving them space while ensuring you never repeat the mistake.

### **Walking in Freedom**

When you proactively fix your side of the street, you clear out the social anxiety that makes school feel unmanageable. You no longer have to avoid specific hallways or worry about who is mad at you. By owning your past and fixing your wreckage, you take your personal power back completely.

Activity: The Repair Roadmap
Look at the 1 to 3 people you listed in Step Eight. For each person, decide the best tool for the repair:

1. **The Direct Conversation:** Is this a situation where a private, face-to-face talk is needed?
   - **The Formula:** "I realized that when I [Action], it was wrong and it hurt you. I want to own that, and I'm going to do [Changed Behavior] from now on."

1. **The Changed Behavior:** Is this a situation where talking would just cause more drama, and it's better to let your actions do the talking?
   - **The Plan:** What specific habit will you permanently stop or start around this person to show you respect them?

1. The Restitution: Did you break, lose, or steal something (including someone's trust or reputation)?
   - **The Plan:** How will you practically replace or rebuild what was lost?
