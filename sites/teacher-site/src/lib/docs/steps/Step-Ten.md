## **“We kept a daily check on our habits and choices, and when we made a mistake, we owned it immediately to keep minor slip-ups from turning into major problems.”**

### **The Five-Minute Clean Up**

Think of the first nine steps like doing a massive, deep clean of a bedroom that has been a disaster for years. Now that your life is clear of that background chaos, you have a choice: wait for it to pile up again, or keep it clean daily. Step Ten is your daily maintenance. It is the habit of checking in with yourself every day to make sure your habits aren't sliding backward.

### **Crushing the Drama Snowball**

Most major high school disasters start as tiny mistakes. A small misunderstanding in a text group turns into a week of silent treatment; a missed assignment turns into a skipped class. When you practice owning your mistakes **promptly**, you kill the drama before it has a chance to breathe. If you catch yourself slipping up, you fix it right then and there.

### **Living Responsibly**

Practicing a daily inventory keeps you firmly in the driver's seat of your high school experience. You don't have to live in fear of getting "caught" or falling behind, because you are constantly making small, honest adjustments every single day. This is how true, lasting autonomy is protected.

## Activity: The End-of-Day Checkpoint

To build this habit, take 60 seconds at the end of every school day to ask yourself two simple questions:

1. **Where did I fight reality today?** Did I try to control something I couldn't, or did I let a bad habit slide? (e.g., "I scrolled on my phone for an hour during study hall.")

1. **Did I create any static?** Do I owe anyone a prompt admission of ownership? (e.g., "I was dismissive to my friend when they asked to sit with us.")

**The Rule:** If you find a slip-up, handle it before school starts tomorrow morning.
