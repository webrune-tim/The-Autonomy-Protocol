## **We became entirely ready to let go of the habits and mindsets that hold us back, committing to the work of replacing them with better ones.**

### **The "Comfortable Cage"**

By now, you’ve named your bad habits. But here is the hard truth: **Sometimes we hold onto bad habits because they are comfortable.** Whether it's the "rush" of a last-minute deadline or the protection of an excuse, we often cling to the very things that keep us stuck. Step Six is about realizing that these "comforts" are actually a cage. To have autonomy, you have to be _entirely ready_ to walk out the door.

### **Willingness vs. Wishing**

There is a big difference between _wishing_ a problem would go away and being _willing_ to let it go.

- **Wishing** is passive. It's hoping things get better without you having to change.
- **Willingness** is active. It means you are ready to give up the "benefits" of your bad habit (like the extra gaming time) to get the "results" of a better life (like passing your classes).

### **Preparing for Change**

Step Six is the "mental prep." It’s the deep breath before the work starts. It’s you telling yourself: _"I am done with the old version of me. I am ready for the version that actually functions."_

## Activity: The "Cost-Benefit" Analysis

Pick one habit from your Accountability Audit (Step Four) that you are struggling to let go of. Answer these two questions honestly:

1. The Benefit: What do I "get" out of this bad habit? (e.g., "I get to avoid hard work," "I get to feel superior to others.")
2. The Cost: What is this habit "stealing" from me? (e.g., "It steals my sleep," "It steals my reputation," "It steals my peace of mind.")

The Decision: Are you ready to give up the "Benefit" in order to stop paying the "Cost"?
