## “We use critical thinking and healthy doubt to evaluate the information, rumors, and internal stories we encounter, while listening deeply to understand the true intent behind the words.”

## Expansion for Students: The Filter of Discernment

As a student in a digital world, you are flooded with information, trends, and opinions 24/7. Everyone is trying to sell you a narrative—social media algorithms tell you how to look, peer groups tell you how to act, and your own worried mind tells you that you aren't doing enough.

- **The Power of Doubt:** Being skeptical doesn't mean being cynical or negative; it means being a scientist of your environment. When you hear a rumor in the hallway or a critical thought in your own head (_"I'm going to fail this entire class"_), Step Five tells you to pause and challenge it: _Is that actually true, or is it just an opinion?_
- **Learning to Listen:** While you challenge the literal words, you must listen to the underlying intent. When a friend acts out, don't believe their words, but listen to their underlying stress or cry for support.
- **The Blueprint:** You don't have to believe everything you hear, and you don't have to believe every thought your brain generates. By combining skepticism with deep listening, you separate truth from illusion and maintain absolute control over what you allow into your mind.
