## “We commit to absolute integrity in how we speak, choosing our words to build clarity, truth, and responsibility, while refusing to participate in gossip, self-sabotage, or false narratives.”

## The Power of the Script

Your word isn't just the noise that comes out of your mouth; it's the tool you use to design your reality. In high school, the "word" is often weaponized through group chats, rumors, and subtle put-downs. Being impeccable means treating your words like high-performance code: precise, functional, and clean.

- **External Integrity:** When you spread gossip or talk trash about a classmate, you aren't just hurting them—you are telling your own brain that your environment is untrustworthy. You leak your own power.
- **Internal Integrity:** The most dangerous lies are the ones you tell yourself. Saying _"I'm just stupid at math"_ or _"Everyone hates me"_ is using your word against yourself.
- **The Blueprint:** An autonomous student says what they mean and means what they say. If you make a promise to a teammate, a teacher, or yourself, you execute it.
